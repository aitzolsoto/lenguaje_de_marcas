Labour leader's call for sensible deal is signal to party that he will resist call to pick sides

Jeremy Corbyn: only Labour will give the people a final say on Brexit

Jeremy Corbyn has set out the four pillars of a “sensible”
Brexit deal he would negotiate with the EU,
as he pledged to carry out whatever the people decide
in a second EU referendum as Labour prime minister.

The Labour leader set out how he would go into an election
offering to negotiate a Brexit deal involving a customs union,
ahead of next week’s party conference where activists will launch
a bid to shift the party’s position towards campaigning to remain in the EU.
